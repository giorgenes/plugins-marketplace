# Visual Identity Design Questions

A targeted question bank for translating brand strategy into actionable visual design decisions.
These questions go deeper than the brand brief's directional cues — the goal is to produce specific,
executable decisions a designer can act on immediately.

**How to use:** Ask conversationally. Extract answers already in the brand brief before asking.
Only ask what remains unanswered. Push for specifics when answers are vague.

---

## 1. Color System

*Goal: arrive at a named palette with clear primary, secondary, accent, and neutral roles.*

- Looking at the brand personality (from the brief), if this brand were a color, what is it?
  What feeling should it trigger in the first half-second?
- Is there a single dominant color the brand should own? Or a palette that always travels together?
- Should the palette feel warm or cool, saturated or muted, bold or quiet?
- What's the background world — mostly light, mostly dark, or flexible? (This defines neutral choices.)
- Are there colors tied to competitors that are off-limits?
- Are there cultural or symbolic color meanings that matter for the brand's geography or audience?
- Name 2–3 brands whose color palette resonates — what do they share?
- What color would feel completely wrong for this brand? Why?

---

## 2. Typography System

*Goal: identify typeface directions (not necessarily final fonts) with clear hierarchy roles.*

- Should the headline voice feel authoritative or approachable? Cold/precise or warm/expressive?
- Serif, sans-serif, or display? Should both be used together, or is one typeface family enough?
- Is the brand primarily digital (screen legibility is critical) or does it live in print/physical media?
- Should the wordmark feel custom/editorial, or standard/neutral (so the mark does the work, not the type)?
- Rate these pairs for the headline typeface — where does this brand land?
  - Geometric ←→ Humanist
  - Condensed ←→ Wide
  - Light/elegant ←→ Heavy/bold
  - Classic ←→ Contemporary
- Is there a specific typeface already in use that must be carried forward, or is this a clean slate?
- Name 2–3 brands or publications whose typography you admire. What makes it work?

---

## 3. Logo & Mark System

*Goal: define the shape of the logo system — what it should and should not be.*

- Should the logo include an icon/symbol, a wordmark, or both?
  - Wordmark only (the name IS the mark — Coca-Cola, Google)
  - Lettermark/monogram (initials — IBM, HBO)
  - Symbol + wordmark (lockup that can separate — Nike, Apple)
  - Emblem (contained badge — Starbucks, Harley)
- Should the symbol be representational (literally depicts something) or abstract (a pure form)?
- Does the brand have or need a short tagline that lives near the logo?
- Must the logo work in a single color (embroidery, stamps, one-color print)?
- What are the primary surfaces the logo will live on? (screen favicon, dark apparel, white packaging, large signage?)
- What shape language should the logo carry — circular, angular, organic, geometric?
- What do you want the logo to communicate before anyone reads the name?
- Name 2–3 logos you admire. What specifically works about them?

---

## 4. Imagery & Photography Style

*Goal: define the photographic and illustrative language of the brand.*

- Does this brand use real photography, illustration, or a mix?
- If photography:
  - Studio-controlled or environmental/in-context?
  - Polished and editorial, or raw and documentary?
  - People-centric or product/concept-centric?
  - Bright and clean, or moody and atmospheric?
  - Color-graded (warm/cool treatment), desaturated, or natural?
  - Should images feature the audience (aspirational mirror) or the product/service?
- If illustration:
  - Flat/geometric, detailed/editorial, textured/handmade, or technical/diagrammatic?
  - Should it feel custom-made or system-consistent?
- What visual styles are off-limits? What would feel wrong or misrepresent the brand?
- Name 3 brands, publications, or campaigns whose visual world resonates. What specifically works?

---

## 5. Shape Language & Form

*Goal: define the geometry and visual character of brand elements, patterns, and UI shapes.*

- Should brand shapes feel geometric (circles, squares, grids) or organic (irregular, free-flowing)?
- Hard corners or rounded corners? If rounded, slightly or very?
- Is there a structural motif that could repeat — a grid, a pattern, a texture?
- Should the brand feel flat (2D, minimal) or have depth (shadows, layers, dimension)?
- Dense and information-rich, or spacious and breathable?
- Should the brand express motion and energy, or stillness and calm?

---

## 6. Layout & Composition Principles

*Goal: define spacing, hierarchy, and compositional values.*

- When a page or screen is laid out, should it feel structured/gridded or loose/editorial?
- Generous whitespace (luxury, premium, calm) or dense content (value, expertise, energy)?
- How many typographic levels should exist in a typical hierarchy? (Headline, subhead, body, caption)
- Is there a primary reading direction — left-to-right dominant, centered, or asymmetric?
- Should the brand feel like it was designed with precision (measured, mathematical) or with feel (intuitive, expressive)?

---

## 7. Motion & Animation Direction (if digital)

*Skip if the brand is primarily physical/print.*

- Should brand motion feel fast/energetic or slow/intentional?
- Mechanical and precise, or organic and natural?
- Is there a signature brand transition or reveal moment?
- Should motion be used sparingly (functional only) or expressively (brand personality)?

---

## 8. Reference Audit

*Use this to sharpen direction by contrast.*

- Name one brand in your category you find visually compelling. What specifically works?
- Name one brand whose visual identity you want to be nothing like. Why?
- Show an image, a website, or a brand system that makes you feel the way you want customers to feel.
- If this brand were a physical object, what would it be? What does it look like? How does it feel to hold?
- If this brand were a physical space — a room, a store, a city — what does it look and feel like?

---

## 9. Constraints & Technical Requirements

- What output formats are needed at launch? (Vector files, PNG exports, web fonts, motion files)
- Are there strict size or format constraints? (App icon at 16px, embroidery minimum, signage maximum)
- Does the brand need to work across multiple languages? Are there non-Latin character sets to consider?
- Is there a design system or UI framework this brand must integrate with?
- Is there a visual lockup with a parent brand or partner that must coexist?
- What is the delivery format expected? (Figma, PDF guidelines, Notion, print-ready files)
